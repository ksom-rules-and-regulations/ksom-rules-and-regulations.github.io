Preliminary

1. Introduction

1.1. Name and Commencement:

These Rules and Regulations shall be called the 'Rules and Regulations of Kerala School of Mathematics' and shall come into effect upon approval by the Government of Kerala.

1.2. Construction and Application:

These Rules and Regulations shall be read, interpreted, and construed in conjunction with the Service Rules of the Kerala School of Mathematics, as may be in force and applicable from time to time.

2. Name and Address of the Institution

2.1. Official Name:

The official name of the institution established hereunder shall be "Kerala School of Mathematics," which may be formally abbreviated as "KSoM."

2.2. Registered Office:

The registered office of the Institute shall be situated at the following address:

Kerala School of Mathematics
Kunnamangalam
Kozhikode - 673571
Kerala, India

3. Definitions

3.1. Definitions:

Unless the context otherwise requires, the following words and abbreviations shall, for the purposes of these Rules and Regulations, have the meanings hereinafter assigned to them:

  1. "Academic Staff" means all teaching, research, and other academic personnel of the Institute, as may be classified by the Executive Council from time to time.
  2. "Act" means The Societies Registration Act, 1860.
  3. "Administrative Staff" means all non-teaching, administrative, technical, and support personnel of the Institute, as may be classified by the Executive Council from time to time.
  4. "Central Government" means the Government of India represented by the Department of Atomic Energy.
  5. "Chairperson" means the Chairperson of the Governing Body of the Institute.
  6. "Competent Authority" means the authority designated for various purposes in these Rules and Regulations or any other rules applicable or in force in the Institute or any such orders issued by the Government or the Central Government for their Autonomous Bodies/Institutions.
  7. "Department" means the Science & Technology Department, Government of Kerala.
  8. "Director" means the Director of the Institute.
  9. "Executive Council" means the Executive Council of the Institute.
  10. "Executive Council Chairperson" means the Chairperson of the Executive Council of the Institute.
  11. "Finance Advisor" means the Internal Financial Advisor of the Institute.
  12. "Governing Body" means the Governing Body of the Institute.
  13. "Government" means the Government of Kerala.
  14. "Institute" means Kerala School of Mathematics.
  15. "NBHM" means the National Board for Higher Mathematics.
  16. "NBHM Chairperson" means the Chairperson of the National Board for Higher Mathematics.
  17. "Registrar" means the Registrar of the Institute.
  18. "Research Council" means the Research Council of the Institute.
  19. "Rules and Regulations" means these Rules and Regulations of the Institute.
  20. "Service Rules" means the Service Rules of the Institute as approved by the Executive Council from time to time.

4. Aims and Objectives of KSoM

The aims and objectives of the Kerala School of Mathematics (KSoM) shall be as follows:

  1. To establish and maintain an environment conducive to the furtherance of creative endeavors, the pursuit of knowledge, and advanced learning within the domains of Mathematical Sciences and associated Theoretical Sciences.
  2. To encourage and execute research and original investigations in fundamental sciences generally, with particular emphasis on Mathematical Sciences.
  3. To cultivate a rigorous mathematical discipline, stimulate enthusiasm for creative work, and foster a spirit of intellectual collaboration among academic professionals engaged in pure and applied branches of science.
  4. To organize lectures, meetings, seminars, and symposia in furtherance of its academic pursuits and for the dissemination of scientific knowledge.
  5. To invite scientists, both domestic and international, engaged in creative work, to deliver presentations and participate in the academic activities of the institution.
  6. To undertake any other measures deemed necessary and beneficial for the advancement of learning in Mathematical Sciences and for the diffusion of knowledge in these sciences.

Constitution of the Governing Board, the Executive Council, and the Research Council of KSoM.

5. Governing Body of KSoM

5.1. Establishment of the Governing Body:

There shall be a Governing Body of the Institute, which shall be the principal governing authority responsible for the overall administration, management, and strategic direction of the Institute.

5.2. Composition of the Governing Body:

The Governing Body shall be constituted as follows:

  1. The Chief Minister, Government of Kerala, who shall serve as the Chairperson.
  2. The Secretary to the Government of Kerala, Science & Technology Department, who shall serve as an Ex-officio Member.
  3. The Secretary to the Government of Kerala, Higher Education Department, who shall serve as an Ex-officio Member
  4. The Secretary to the Government of Kerala, Department of Finance, who shall serve as an Ex-officio Member.
  5. The Chairperson of the National Board for Higher Mathematics (NBHM), who shall serve as an Ex-officio Member.
  6. The Vice Chancellor of the Homi Bhabha National Institute, who shall serve as an Ex-officio Member.
  7. The Director of the Institute of Mathematical Sciences (IMSc), Chennai, who shall serve as an Ex-officio Member.
  8. The Director of the Chennai Mathematical Institute, who shall serve as an Ex-officio Member.
  9. Five (5) eminent academicians, including not less than three (3) mathematicians of international stature, who shall be nominated by the Government from a panel of experts recommended by the NBHM Chairperson, and shall serve as Members.
  10. The Director of KSoM, who shall serve as the Member Secretary.
  11. The Registrar of KSoM shall attend meetings to provide secretarial support.

5.3. Tenure of Nominated Members:

The tenure of nominated members of the Governing Body, excluding Ex-officio Members, shall be three (3) years. The Government shall effect the reconstitution of the Governing Body not less than one (1) month prior to the expiry of the said tenure.

5.4. Continuation in Office:

Notwithstanding the provisions of Clause 5.3, the Governing Body shall continue to hold office beyond the expiry of its three-year tenure until such time as it is duly reconstituted by the Government.

5.5. Tenure of Ex-officio Members:

The term of office for Ex-officio Members shall be co-terminus with the duration of the official position by virtue of which they are appointed to the Governing Body.

5.6. Resignation of Members:

Any member of the Governing Body, other than an Ex-officio Member, may resign from membership by submitting a written letter of resignation to the Director. Such resignation shall become effective upon the date of its formal acceptance by the Director.

5.7. Filling of Vacancies:

When a vacancy arises in the office of a member of the Governing Body nominated under clause 5.2(9) by reason of death, resignation, or any other cause, such vacancy shall be filled by the Government by nominating a replacement member from a panel of experts recommended by the NBHM Chairperson. A member nominated to fill such a casual vacancy shall hold office for the remaining duration of the current term of the Governing Body.

5.8. Powers and Functions:

  1. To approve the annual budget, annual plan, and vision statement of the Institute.
  2. To conduct annual reviews of the operational performance and functioning of the Institute.
  3. To consider policy matters adopted by the Executive Council of the Institute and to provide broad guidance to the Institute.

5.9. Meetings:

The Governing Body shall convene for a meeting not less than once in each calendar year.

6. Executive Council of KSoM

6.1. Constitution:

The Executive Council shall be constituted as follows:

  1. The Secretary to the Government of Kerala, Science & Technology Department, who shall serve as the Chairperson.
  2. The Chairperson of the National Board for Higher Mathematics (NBHM), who shall serve as a Member.
  3. The Chairperson of the Research Council, who shall serve as a Member.
  4. Three (3) eminent academicians nominated by the Governing Body from among the members appointed under Clause 5.2(9), who shall serve as Members.
  5. The Secretary to the Government of Kerala, Higher Education Department, who shall serve as a Member.
  6. The Secretary to the Government of Kerala, Department of Finance, who shall serve as a Member.
  7. The Director, who shall serve as the Member Secretary.
  8. The Registrar, who shall serve as the Member Convenor.

6.2. Tenure of Members:

The term of office for the Chairperson and for members nominated to the Executive Council in their individual capacity shall be three (3) years. The term of office for Ex-officio Members shall be co-terminus with the duration of the official position by virtue of which they are appointed to the Executive Council.

6.3. Meetings and Procedures:

The meetings of the Executive Council shall be convened not less than twice in each calendar year by the Chairperson of the Executive Council on a date and at a time as may be determined by the Director in consultation with the Executive Council Chairperson. For the purposes of this clause, each year shall be deemed to commence on the first day of January and conclude on the thirty-first day of December of the same year. Meetings of the Executive Council shall ordinarily be held at the Institute unless the Executive Council Chairperson directs otherwise.

6.4. Special Meetings:

A special meeting of the Executive Council may be requisitioned at any time by the Chairperson, either on the Chairperson's own initiative or upon the written request of not less than four (4) members of the Executive Council.

6.5. Notice of Meetings:

  1. For any meeting of the Executive Council, a notice of not less than fifteen (15) days shall be provided to the members. The non-receipt of notice of any meeting of the Executive Council by any member shall not, however, invalidate the proceedings of the meeting.
  2. A notice specifying the date, time, and place of every meeting shall be dispatched in writing to the members by the Member Secretary of the Executive Council. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Chairperson may convene a special meeting of the Executive Council on shorter notice to consider urgent matters.
  3. Notices may be delivered by hand, dispatched by registered post or speed post to the address of each member as recorded in the official records of the Executive Council, or transmitted by official electronic mail. Notices so dispatched shall be deemed to have been duly delivered at the time at which notice would be delivered in the ordinary course of post or electronic transmission.
  4. The agenda for the meeting shall be circulated by the Secretary of the Executive Council not less than ten (10) days prior to the meeting.
  5. Notices of motions for the inclusion of any item on the agenda must be received by the Secretary of the Executive Council not less than one (1) week prior to the meeting. The Chairperson may, at the Chairperson's discretion, permit the inclusion of any item for which due notice was not provided.

6.6. Chairperson of the Meeting:

The Chairperson of the Executive Council shall preside at all meetings of the Executive Council. In the event of the absence of the Chairperson, the members present shall either elect a Chairperson from amongst themselves to preside at that specific meeting, or the nominee of the Chairperson for the particular meeting shall preside over the meeting and shall exercise all the rights and privileges of the Chairperson for that meeting.

6.7. Quorum and Procedure:

  1. A quorum for any meeting of the Executive Council shall consist of six (6) members, which number must include the person presiding over the meeting.
  2. All matters brought before a meeting of the Executive Council for consideration shall be determined by the mutual consent of all members present. In the event of a difference of opinion amongst the members, the decision of the majority shall be conclusive.
  3. Each member of the Executive Council, including the person presiding, shall be entitled to one vote. Should there be an equality of votes on any matter requiring determination by the Executive Council, the person presiding shall, in addition to their original vote, have and exercise a casting vote.
  4. The ruling of the person presiding shall be final and binding with respect to all questions concerning the procedure for the conduct of the meeting.

6.8. Failure to Attend Meetings:

If a member of the Executive Council, excepting official representatives of Government, fails to attend three (3) consecutive meetings of the Executive Council without obtaining leave of absence from the Executive Council, that member shall thereupon cease to be a member of the Executive Council.

6.9. Absence of Official Representatives:

In the event that the Secretary to the Government of Kerala responsible for matters pertaining to the Institute, or the Secretary to the Government of Kerala, Department of Finance, are unable to attend a meeting of the Executive Council, they may be represented by their duly authorized representative nominee(s). Such representative nominee(s) shall not hold a rank lower than that of a Director or Deputy Secretary to the Government. In such instances, the representative nominee shall possess full authorization and shall be deemed a Member for the duration of that particular Executive Council meeting, and shall be included for the purpose of constituting a quorum.

6.10. Financial Matters and Approvals:

  1. Employment structure proposals: Proposals pertaining to the employment structure, that is to say, the adoption, revision, and allowances of pay scales, and the creation of posts, shall require the prior approval of the Government.
  2. Disagreement on Financial Matters: In the event of a disagreement arising between the Secretary to the Government of Kerala, Department of Finance, and the Chairperson of the Executive Council of KSoM concerning any financial matter exceeding the delegated authority of the relevant Ministry or Department of the Government, the said matter may be referred to the Chief Minister for a determination in accordance with the established procedure.

6.11. Adjourned Meeting:

  1. If a quorum is not present at the expiration of fifteen (15) minutes following the time appointed for a meeting, the meeting shall stand adjourned to such date and time as the Chairperson may determine.
  2. Notwithstanding the foregoing, if a meeting is adjourned due to the absence of a quorum after the expiration of fifteen (15) minutes, it may be reconvened on the same day after an interval of thirty (30) minutes, as the Chairperson may determine.
  3. No quorum shall be required for a meeting that has been adjourned for want of a quorum.

6.12. Minutes of the Meeting:

The minutes of each meeting, together with any amendments thereto, shall be presented for confirmation at the subsequent meeting of the Executive Council. Upon such confirmation and signature by the Chairperson, the minutes shall be duly recorded in the minutes book, which shall thereafter be available for inspection by the members of the Executive Council at all times.

6.13. Business by Circulation:

Any business of the Institute required to be transacted by the Executive Council, save and except such business as is specifically required to be transacted at a meeting of the Executive Council, may be transacted by circulation amongst all its members. Any resolution so circulated and approved by a majority of the members who have responded thereto shall be as effective and binding as if such resolution had been duly passed at a meeting of the Executive Council. Every resolution so approved by circulation shall be reported at the subsequent meeting of the Executive Council for information and ratification.

6.14. Vacancies:

When a vacancy arises in the office of a member of the Executive Council, other than the Director, by reason of death, resignation, or any other cause, such vacancy shall be filled by the authority responsible for the appointment of the member in whose office the vacancy has arisen. A member nominated to fill such a casual vacancy shall hold office for the remainder of the term of the member in whose place that member has been nominated. No act, proceeding, or resolution of the Executive Council shall be rendered invalid by reason of any vacancy in the membership of the Executive Council or any defect in the appointment of any person acting as a member thereof.

6.15. Committees of the Executive Council:

The Executive Council may constitute committees comprising individuals selected from amongst its own members, the staff of the Institute, or outside experts, or any combination thereof. The Executive Council may delegate to such committees such powers and assign to them such duties as are consistent with the provisions of these Rules and Regulations and any other applicable rules.

6.16. Authentication of Orders and Decisions:

All orders and decisions of the Executive Council shall be authenticated by the signature of the Registrar, or by any other person duly authorized by the Executive Council for that purpose.

6.17. Disqualification:

  1. Unsoundness of mind, as determined by a competent medical authority.
  2. Conviction by a court of competent jurisdiction for an offence involving moral turpitude.
  3. Adjudication as an insolvent.
  4. Submission and acceptance of a formal resignation from membership.
  5. Demise.

7. Powers and Functions of the Executive Council

7.1. General Powers and Functions:

  1. To supervise the administration and management of the Institute.
  2. To consider budget estimates proposed by the Institute and make recommendations thereon to the Governing Body.
  3. To exercise financial powers as stipulated in the schedule appended to these Rules and Regulations, including, but not limited to, the consideration of proposals for new and additional expenditure.
  4. To formulate rules specific to the mandate of the Institute and to determine the duties of the Director and other employees of the Institute in alignment with the Institute's mandate.
  5. To frame and revise rules governing the Institute.
  6. To approve the annual report and the annual audited statement of accounts of the Institute within six (6) months of the conclusion of each financial year, and to submit the same to the Government.
  7. To frame rules pertaining to the establishment, pay, pension, disciplinary proceedings, and other related matters concerning the employees of the Institute.
  8. To consider proposals from the Institute regarding changes in the intake of students and postdoctoral fellows, commensurate with the mandate of the Institute.
  9. To promote the mandate of the Institute, including research and other activities consistent with the said mandate.
  10. To consider all cases of promotion and to grant promotion to all employees in accordance with the promotion rules of the Institute.
  11. To frame rules governing recruitment, promotion policy, pay structure, and cadre structure for positions below the level of Director of the Institute. The appointment of the Director of the Institute shall be effected by the Executive Council with the approval of the Government in adherence to guidelines issued by the Government.
  12. To make recommendations to the Government concerning the creation of new positions or the upgradation of existing positions, recognizing that the authority for the creation or upgradation of positions vests with the Government. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the creation of posts to accommodate the promotion of scientific, technical, faculty, or other personnel through personal promotion may be approved by the Executive Council.
  13. To oversee the execution of contracts and other instruments on behalf of the Institute, the conduct and defense of legal proceedings, and to determine the manner of signing and pleading. The Executive Council may re-delegate these powers to such other officers as it deems necessary.
  14. To formulate such rules as may be necessary for the fulfillment of the objectives of the Institute and pursuant to any rule of the Institute.
  15. To appoint members of the Finance Committee.
  16. To appoint Statutory Auditors and determine their remuneration.
  17. To delegate its powers to the Director.
  18. The Executive Council shall be competent to conduct a Cadre Review of the Administrative and Auxiliary personnel of the Institute and to approve the same for implementation. Any creation of posts, the pay of which is equivalent to or exceeds that of a Joint Secretary to the Government, arising from such cadre review shall be referred to the department for approval after adherence to necessary procedures.

8. Research Council of KSoM

8.1. Constitution of the Research Council:

The Research Council shall be constituted as follows:

  1. The Chairperson of the National Board for Higher Mathematics (NBHM) or a nominee designated by the NBHM Chairperson, who shall serve as the Chairperson of the Research Council.
  2. The Director of the Institute, who shall serve as a Member.
  3. Seven (7) eminent academicians nominated by the Governing Body from a panel of experts recommended by the NBHM Chairperson. In the event that the Governing Body does not approve any recommendation from the panel submitted by the NBHM Chairperson, the Governing Body may request the NBHM Chairperson to submit a revised panel. The nominated academicians shall serve as Members.
  4. The Dean Academic of KSoM, who shall serve as the Member Convenor.

8.2. Tenure and Appointment of Members:

The term of office for the Chairperson and for members nominated to the Research Council in their individual capacity shall be three (3) years. The term of office for Ex-officio Members shall be co-terminus with the duration of the official position by virtue of which they are appointed to the Research Council.

8.3. Role, Objectives, Powers, and Functions:

  1. The primary role of the Research Council is to advise and monitor the Institute in research and academic matters and to provide counsel on measures to elevate the Institute.
  2. The Research Council shall also advise or make recommendations to the Executive Council on matters involving policies and methodologies of academic and research matters, including recruitments, which shall be forwarded to the Executive Council for final consideration, and upon which the Executive Council shall base its decisions.
  3. The Research Council shall advise on faculty development and evaluation, including the establishment of standards and processes for faculty evaluation, promotion, and tenure, ensuring alignment with the Institute's research goals.
  4. The Research Council shall develop and oversee policies and procedures related to research ethics and integrity, including guidelines for the responsible conduct of research, conflict of interest, and the handling of allegations of research misconduct.
  5. The Research Council shall facilitate interdisciplinary research and collaboration within the Institute and with external institutions, including identifying potential areas for collaboration, organizing workshops and conferences.
  6. The Research Council shall advise on strategies to enhance the Institute's research visibility and impact, both nationally and internationally, including recommending ways to improve research dissemination and support knowledge transfer activities.
  7. The Research Council shall advise on the development and maintenance of research infrastructure and resources, including laboratories, equipment, and computational resources, and make recommendations on the allocation of resources to support research activities.
  8. The Research Council shall evaluate the effectiveness of the Institute's research programs and initiatives, and recommend improvements to enhance their quality, impact, and efficiency.
  9. The Research Council shall advise on matters related to research funding and resource allocation, including identifying potential sources of funding, and developing guidelines for the allocation of research funds within the Institute.

8.4. Meetings and Procedures:

The meetings of the Research Council shall be convened not less than twice in each calendar year by the Chairperson of the Research Council on a date and at a time as may be determined by the Director in consultation with the Research Council Chairperson. For the purposes of this clause, each year shall be deemed to commence on the first day of January and conclude on the thirty-first day of December of the same year. Meetings of the Research Council shall ordinarily be held at the Institute unless the Research Council Chairperson directs otherwise.

8.5. Business by Circulation:

Any business of the Research Council required to be transacted, except such business as is specifically required to be transacted at a meeting of the Research Council, may be transacted by circulation amongst all its members. Any resolution so circulated and approved by a majority of the members who have responded thereto shall be as effective and binding as if such resolution had been duly passed at a meeting of the Research Council. Every resolution so approved by circulation shall be reported at the subsequent meeting of the Research Council for information and ratification.

8.6. Vacancies:

When a vacancy arises in the office of a member of the Research Council, other than the Director, by reason of death, resignation, or any other cause, such vacancy shall be filled by the authority responsible for the appointment of the member in whose office the vacancy has arisen. A member nominated to fill such a casual vacancy shall hold office for the remainder of the term of the member in whose place that member has been nominated. No act, proceeding, or resolution of the Research Council shall be rendered invalid by reason of any vacancy in the membership of the Research Council or any defect in the appointment of any person acting as a member thereof.

8.7. Committees of the Research Council:

The Research Council may constitute committees comprising individuals selected from amongst its own members, the staff of the Institute, or outside experts, or any combination thereof. The Research Council may delegate to such committees such powers and assign to them such duties as are consistent with the provisions of these Rules and Regulations and any other applicable rules.

8.8. Disqualification:

  1. Unsoundness of mind, as determined by a competent medical authority.
  2. Conviction by a court of competent jurisdiction for an offence involving moral turpitude.
  3. Adjudication as an insolvent.
  4. Submission and acceptance of a formal resignation from membership.
  5. Demise.

Powers of the Executive Council & Other Officials

9. Service Extension

9.1. Age of Superannuation:

The age of superannuation for employees of the Institute shall be as follows, as determined and governed by the prevailing Rules and Regulations of the Institute, as amended from time to time:

  1. For Academic Staff: sixty (60) years.
  2. For Administrative Staff: fifty-six (56) years.

9.2. Grant of Extension of Service:

Subject to the provisions hereinafter contained, the Executive Council of the Institute may, in its absolute discretion, grant extensions of service to members of the Academic Staff who possess demonstrated exceptional merit in their respective disciplines.

9.3. Consideration for Extension:

The grant of an extension of service shall not, under any circumstances, be deemed to be a matter of right or routine. Consideration for extension shall be restricted to a specified proportion of the faculty members who attain the age of superannuation during a period designated for such consideration. The said proportion and the period of consideration shall be determined and notified by the Executive Council from time to time.

9.4. Initial Extension Term:

Any initial extension of service granted pursuant to this Policy shall be for a fixed term not exceeding two (2) years.

9.5. Further Extension:

Following a comprehensive review and evaluation, a further extension may be granted for an additional term not exceeding two (2) years.

9.6. Maximum Age for Extension:

Notwithstanding anything contained in this Policy, no extension of service shall be granted to any member of the Academic Staff beyond the age of sixty-four (64) years.

9.7. Executive Council Decision:

All proposals for the grant of extension of service shall be subject to the exclusive consideration and final decision of the Executive Council.

10. Appointing Authorities

10.1. Executive Council as Appointing Authority:

The Executive Council shall serve as the designated Appointing Authority for all appointments to positions classified in Pay Matrix Level 13 and above within the Institute.

10.2. Director as Appointing Authority:

The Director shall serve as the designated Appointing Authority for all appointments to positions classified in Pay Matrix Level 12 and below within the Institute.

11. Approval of Promotions

11.1. Promotion Approval:

The promotion of employees to a particular Pay Matrix Level shall be subject to the approval of the authority designated as the Appointing Authority for positions classified in that specific Pay Matrix Level, as stipulated in clauses 10.1 and 10.2 of these Rules and Regulations.

12. Disciplinary Authority

12.1. For Employees Appointed by the Executive Council:

The Executive Council shall be the competent authority to initiate disciplinary proceedings and impose penalties upon employees for whom the Executive Council serves as the Appointing Authority. Decisions rendered by the Executive Council in such matters shall be final and no appeal shall lie against such orders. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Executive Council may, at its discretion, entertain representations submitted by the concerned employees for reconsideration of the imposed penalties.

12.2. For Employees Appointed by the Director:

The Director of the Institute shall be the Disciplinary Authority for employees for whom the Director serves as the Appointing Authority. In all such cases, the Executive Council shall function as the Appellate Authority, as well as the Revisionary and Review Authority.

12.3. For Administrative Staff:

The Registrar of the Institute shall be the Disciplinary Authority for members of the Administrative Staff. In such cases, the Director of the Institute shall serve as the Appellate Authority, and the Executive Council shall serve as the Revisionary and Review Authority.

13. Specific Powers of the Authorities

13.1. Power to Fix Initial Pay:

The Chairperson of the Executive Council is hereby vested with the authority to determine and fix the initial emoluments of an incumbent at a stage exceeding the minimum of the stipulated pay scale, by granting a maximum of five (5) increments. This power shall be exercised solely upon receipt of a recommendation from the duly constituted Academic Selection Committee, and shall be applicable only in respect of those posts for which the Executive Councilis the designated Appointing Authority.

13.2. Exercise of Power by the Chairperson:

Notwithstanding the requirement for Executive Council approval, the Chairperson may, in circumstances deemed to be in the best interest of the Institute, exercise the aforementioned power on behalf of the Executive Council. Any action so taken by the Chairperson shall be reported to the Executive Council at its next ensuing meeting for ratification.

14. Director

14.1. Position and General Powers:

The Director shall be the principal executive and academic officer and the Head of the Institute. The Director shall be empowered to exercise the powers of the Head of a Department as stipulated under the Fundamental Rules and Supplementary Rules (FR & SRs), General Financial Rules, Travelling Allowance Rules (TA Rules), Leave Travel Concession Rules (LTC Rules), Provident Fund Rules (both Contributory and General), and other applicable Financial Rules, as amended from time to time.

14.2. Duties and Adherence to Rules:

It shall be incumbent upon the Director to ensure the proper and efficient functioning of the Institute in strict compliance with these Rules and Regulations and any other rules or policies of the Institute, and to act under the general direction and control of the Executive Council.

14.3. Supervision, Control, and Emergency Powers:

Save as otherwise expressly provided by the rules and these Rules and Regulations, the Director shall possess all necessary powers for the exercise of general supervision and control over all aspects of the Institute's work and for the effective and efficient management thereof. In circumstances of an emergent nature, the Director shall be authorized to take such action as deemed necessary and shall report the same to the Executive Council at its next subsequent meeting for information and, where necessary, ratification.

14.4. Direction and Control over Staff:

All members of the staff of the Institute, and all other individuals engaged in work at the Institute, including those employed on research projects or schemes funded by external agencies, irrespective of whether their salaries are disbursed by the Institute or directly by such agencies, shall be under the general direction and control of the Director.

14.5. Authority for Recruitment and Promotion:

The Director shall be responsible for the recruitment and promotion of personnel in respect of those posts for which the Director is designated as the Appointing Authority, as specified in clause 10.2 of these Rules and Regulations.

14.6. Power to Fix Initial Pay (Director's Appointing Authority Level):

The Director shall have the authority to fix the initial pay of an incumbent, for posts where the Director is the Appointing Authority, at a stage above the minimum of the applicable pay scale, by granting up to five (5) increments. This power shall be exercised upon the recommendation of the relevant Selection Committee constituted for the specific category of appointment (academic or administrative). Such fixation shall conform to the norms stipulated by the Government of India (GoI) / Department of Atomic Energy (DAE) for academic staff and by the Government of Kerala for administrative personnel, as applicable.

14.7. Power to Engage Temporary Personnel:

The Director is empowered to engage temporary personnel, including Project Staff, Project Assistants, Technicians, and Consultants, as operational requirements necessitate. Such engagements shall ensure remuneration not less than mandated minimum wages and shall not exceed the relevant project or assignment duration. Applicable norms of the Government of India (GoI) / Department of Atomic Energy (DAE) for academic staff and the Government of Kerala for administrative personnel shall be strictly adhered to.

14.8. Authority for Academic Travel:

Subject to such terms and conditions as the Executive Council may, from time to time, stipulate, the Director shall be empowered to authorize national and international travel for academic staff, doctoral candidates, and postdoctoral researchers for purposes including, but not limited to, academic visits and attendance at conferences.

14.9. Powers Regarding Buildings:

The Director shall have the authority to take any building on hire or lease out any building belonging to the Institute.

14.10. Power to Sanction Rent Remission/Reduction:

The Director shall be empowered to sanction the remission or reduction of rent for buildings or parts thereof rendered wholly or partially unsuitable for occupation.

14.11. Powers of Estate Officer:

The Director shall exercise the powers vested in an "Estate Officer" in relation to the office and residential buildings of the Institute.

14.12. Assignment of Registrar's Duties (Short Absence):

During the temporary absence of the Registrar, not exceeding a period of one (1) month, the Director may assign any or all of the functions of the Registrar to any other employee(s) of the Institute as deemed appropriate.

14.13. Assignment of Registrar's Duties (Extended Absence):

In the event of the Registrar's absence exceeding a period of one (1) month, the Director shall obtain authorization from the Executive Council to assign responsibility for the Registrar's functions as provided in clause 14.12 for a period exceeding one month.

14.14. Assumption of Duties During Director's Absence:

During the Director's absence from the headquarters, the Director may authorize one of the Deans or, from amongst the Professors present who have not attained the age of superannuation, the Professor of highest academic standing among those available, to assume and execute the current duties of the office of the Director.

14.15. Delegation of Powers by the Director:

The Director may, subject to the prior approval of the Executive Council, delegate any of the powers, duties, and authorities vested in the office of the Director to one or more members of the academic or administrative staff of the Institute.

14.16. Responsibility for Implementation of Reservation Policies:

The Director shall be responsible for the due and effective implementation of all applicable statutory provisions, rules, regulations, and executive instructions governing reservation in public employment for Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), Other Backward Classes (OBC), and persons with disabilities.

14.17. Implementation of Judicial Pronouncements:

The Director shall possess the requisite authority to determine and effectuate the implementation or non-implementation of judgements, decrees, or orders pronounced or issued by competent courts of law pertaining to the employees of the Institute.

14.18. Engagement of Legal Counsel:

The Director shall have the authority to engage legal counsel/advocates/lawyers to represent the Institute in legal proceedings and to sanction the payment of their fees.

15. Registrar

15.1. Custodianship of Seal and Estate:

The Registrar shall, by virtue of office, be the lawful custodian of the Official Seal of the Institute and the estate thereof.

15.2. Secretarial Support to Executive Council:

The Registrar shall be responsible for providing necessary secretarial support and assistance to the Executive Council.

15.3. Reporting and Control:

The Registrar shall be subordinate to the Director and shall perform all duties and functions under the general supervision and direction of the Director. In all matters pertaining to the affairs of the Institute, the Registrar shall act in accordance with the general control and specific directives issued by the Director.

15.4. Responsibility for Correspondence:

The Registrar shall be responsible for managing all correspondence related to the Institute, subject to and in accordance with the instructions issued by the Director from time to time.

15.5. Charge of Staff and Maintenance:

The Registrar shall have charge of the administrative and non-academic staff, as well as the general maintenance staff of the Institute.

15.6. Maintenance and Upkeep of Premises:

The Registrar shall be responsible for overseeing the maintenance and upkeep of the Institute's premises.

Staff, Categories and Appointments

16. Classification

16.1. Classification of Employees:

Employees of the Institute shall be classified into distinct groups based upon the Pay Matrix Levels assigned to their respective positions. The specific criteria and equivalence for classifying positions within the Pay Matrix Levels into Group "A", Group "B", and Group "C" shall be determined by the applicable norms governing the classification of Academic Staff and Administrative Staff, as stipulated in the subsequent clauses of these Rules and Regulations.

16.2. Group 'A' Classification for Academic Staff:

All employees holding positions classified as Academic Staff, carrying emoluments in the Pay Matrix at Level 10 up to and including Level 18, shall be categorised as Group "A".

16.3. Classification of Administrative Staff:

Administrative Staff shall be classified into Group "A", Group "B", and Group "C" in strict adherence to the classification rules and criteria stipulated by the Government of Kerala, as amended from time to time.

16.4. Amendments to Classification:

The classification of Academic Staff as Group "A", as set forth in clause 16.2, shall be subject to any relevant amendments made to the classification of Group "A" posts in the Government of India Service by the Government of India. The classification of Administrative Staff into Group "A", "B", and "C", as set forth in clause 16.3, shall be subject to any amendments made to the classification rules and criteria by the Government of Kerala.

17. Appointments & Promotions

17.1. Filling of Posts:

All vacant posts within the Institute shall be filled strictly in accordance with the recruitment and promotion rules duly approved by the Executive Council of the Institute, as amended from time to time.

17.2. Reservation of Posts:

While undertaking the process of making appointments, the Institute shall ensure the necessary provision for the reservation of posts in favour of Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), Other Backward Classes (OBC), and Persons with Disabilities, in strict compliance with the decisions and directives issued by the Executive Council.

17.3. Appointment of Overseas Citizens of India (OCI):

The Executive Council is hereby empowered to frame rules or procedures governing the appointment of Overseas Citizens of India (OCI) within the Institute, provided that such rules or procedures are in conformity with the regulations and guidelines issued by the Government.

17.4. Reporting to Executive Council:

A formal report detailing all appointments made and promotions granted shall be submitted to the Executive Council at its next ensuing meeting for information.

18. Appointment of the Director

18.1. Constitution of Search Committee:

A Search Committee shall be constituted by the Government upon receipt of a formal request from the Executive Council.

18.2. Consideration of Search Committee Recommendation:

The recommendation submitted by the Search Committee shall be placed before the Executive Council for its due consideration and ratification.

18.3. Term of Appointment:

The initial term of appointment for the Director shall ordinarily not exceed five (5) years.

18.4. Salary, Allowances, and Conditions of Service:

The salary, allowances, and other conditions of service applicable to the Director shall be determined and fixed by the Executive Council in accordance with the norms provided by the S&T Department.

18.5. Eligibility for Re-employment:

The incumbent Director shall be eligible for re-appointment for one or more further terms, each not exceeding five (5) years, with the concurrence of the Government. Notwithstanding such eligibility, no individual shall continue to hold the office of Director after having attained the age of sixty-five (65) years.

18.6. Resignation or Early Relief:

Any matter concerning the acceptance of the resignation of the Director or the early relief of the Director from duty shall be referred to the Science & Technology Department, Government of Kerala, for necessary action.

19. Officiating and Acting Director

19.1. Appointment of Officiating Director:

Notwithstanding anything contained in clause 16 of these Rules and Regulations, in the event of the absence of the duly appointed Director for a continuous period exceeding thirty (30) days, the Chairperson of the Executive Council may appoint a person to officiate as Director. Any such appointment shall be reported to the Executive Council at its next ensuing meeting for information. The person so appointed to officiate shall be responsible for the day-to-day administration of the Institute and shall not exercise the statutory powers vested in the regular Director appointed under clause 16.

19.2. Temporary Appointment of Acting Director:

In the event of a vacancy in the office of the Director, the Executive Council may make a temporary appointment of an Acting Director. Any appointment made under this clause shall be for a period not exceeding one (1) year at a time and shall be subject to the concurrence of the Government.

20. Appointment of Registrar

20.1. Appointment of Registrar:

The appointment to the positions of Registrar shall be made by the Executive Council. Such appointments shall be effected upon the formal recommendation of the Director, irrespective of the Pay Matrix Level assigned to the said positions.

Finance & Accounts

21. Budget & Funds of the Institute

21.1. Financial Year:

The financial year of the Institute shall commence on the 1st day of April and conclude on the 31st day of March of the succeeding calendar year.

21.2. Preparation and Approval of Annual Budget Estimates:

The Director shall be responsible for the preparation of the annual budget estimates of the Institute and shall obtain the approval of the Executive Council for the same.

21.3. Reporting of Financial Information:

Information pertaining to the receipts and expenditure of the Institute shall be presented in accordance with the following categories:

  1. Actuals for the preceding financial year;
  2. Approved budget estimates for the current financial year;
  3. Revised Estimates for the current financial year;
  4. Budget Estimates for the forthcoming financial year.

21.4. Constitution of Institute Funds:

The funds of the Institute shall comprise the following:

  1. Grants received from governments, governmental bodies, universities, institutes, corporations, institutions, or societies;
  2. Income and benefits derived or accruing from the properties, assets, and investments of the Institute, the proceeds from the sale of the Institute's publications, fees levied from time to time, user charges, and other similar sources;
  3. Contributions or grants received from such other sources as are approved by the Executive Council;
  4. Income generated from consultancy services and other related charges;
  5. Corpus Funds.

21.5. Maintenance of Accounts:

The accounts of the Institute shall be maintained in strict conformity with the applicable Accounting Standards and in the format prescribed by law.

21.6. Responsibility for Accounts:

The Registrar shall be responsible for the maintenance and oversight of the accounts of the Institute.

21.7. Authorization of Payments:

All bills submitted for payment shall bear a clear endorsement stating "Passed for payment" and shall be signed by the Director or by an Officer of the Institute to whom such power has been duly delegated.

21.8. Banking Arrangements:

All moneys received for or on behalf of the Institute shall be deposited in the name of the Institute in current, savings, or fixed deposit accounts maintained with a nationalised bank or any other bank as may be notified by the Ministry of Finance.

22. Audit

22.1. Annual Audit Requirement:

The accounts of the Institute shall be subject to an annual audit conducted by duly qualified Chartered Accountant(s), hereinafter referred to as the "Statutory Auditors," as mandated by applicable law.

22.2. Appointment of Statutory Auditors:

The Statutory Auditors shall be appointed by the Director with the prior approval of the Executive Council. Such appointment shall be made from time to time for the purpose of conducting the annual audit.

23. Exercise of Financial Powers

23.1. Governance by Government Orders:

The exercise of financial powers within the Institute, including but not limited to matters relating to Purchases, Works Contracts, Consultancy Contracts, and other related transactions, shall be governed by and conducted in strict accordance with the orders and directives issued by the Government from time to time concerning the Delegation of Financial Powers.

23.2. Delegation of Powers by Executive Council:

The Executive Council shall be empowered to delegate its financial powers to the various authorities within the Institute to such extent as deemed necessary and appropriate for the efficient functioning of the Institute.

Committees

24. Finance Committee

24.1. Constitution of the Finance Committee:

There shall be constituted a Finance Committee of the Institute comprising the following members:

  1. The Chairperson of the Executive Council, who shall serve as the ex-officio Chairperson of the Finance Committee.
  2. The Director of the Institute.
  3. The Secretary to the Government of Kerala, Department of Finance.
  4. One additional member nominated by the Executive Council.

24.2. Functions of the Finance Committee:

The functions of the Finance Committee shall be as follows:

  1. To examine and scrutinise the annual budget estimates of the Institute and submit its recommendations thereon to the Executive Council.
  2. To examine the annual financial statements of the Institute.
  3. To examine any matter or issue related to the finances of the Institute as may be referred to it.
  4. To determine and review user charges.
  5. To exercise such financial powers of the Executive Council as may be delegated to it concerning the finalisation of consultancy contracts, works contracts, and procurement contracts.

24.3. Meetings:

The Finance Committee shall convene meetings as frequently as deemed necessary or as desired by the Executive Council or the Chairperson of the Finance Committee for the purpose of addressing any specific task.

24.4. Submission of Minutes:

The Committee shall submit the minutes of its meetings to the Executive Council for consideration and ratification.

25. Building and Works Committee (BWC)

25.1. Constitution of the Building and Works Committee:

There shall be constituted a Building and Works Committee (BWC) of the Institute by the Executive Council. The Committee shall comprise not less than five (5) and not more than seven (7) members, who shall be nominated or constituted by the Executive Council, and shall function under the chairpersonship of the Director of the Institute.

25.2. Composition:

The composition of the Building and Works Committee shall include representatives from the Administration and Finance departments of the Government.

25.3. Functioning and Oversight of Works:

The Committee shall function under the direction of the Executive Council. The Committee shall oversee the planning, execution, and completion of all building and works projects of the Institute. In discharging its functions, the Committee shall ensure strict adherence to the procedures stipulated for Public Works by the Central Public Works Department (CPWD) and the prevailing Government Orders issued by the Government of Kerala governing the execution of public works through accredited agencies in the State.

26. Academic Selection Committee

26.1. Constitution and Functions:

The Academic Selection Committee, responsible for matters pertaining to the recruitment and promotion of academic staff, shall be constituted and shall function in strict conformity with the recruitment and promotion rules of the Institute, as duly approved by the Executive Council from time to time.

Service Conditions

27. Disciplinary Rules

27.1. Authority for Disciplinary Action:

The authority vested with the power to appoint a member of the staff shall also be competent to suspend, discharge, dismiss, or otherwise impose penalties upon such staff member for reasons of misconduct or breach of the terms and conditions governing their appointment.

27.2. Governing Conduct and Disciplinary Rules:

In matters pertaining to conduct and disciplinary proceedings, the employees of the Institute shall be governed by the Institute's own Conduct and Disciplinary Rules, as duly adopted by the Executive Council. These rules and Regulations shall be aligned with the principles and provisions of the Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964, and the Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965.

27.3. Application of Service Rules:

The Institute shall apply and adhere to the Service Rules, as approved by the Executive Council and currently in force, including but not limited to the Fundamental Rules, Supplementary Rules, Travelling Allowance Rules, Leave Rules, Leave Travel Concession Rules, and Pension Rules, in matters concerning the service conditions of its employees.

28. Leave Rules

28.1. Grant of Leave:

Employees of the Institute shall be eligible for and granted various forms of leave, including but not limited to casual leave, earned leave, and leave on medical grounds, in accordance with the leave rules of the Institute as formally adopted. For members of the Academic Staff, the said rules shall be aligned with the principles and provisions of the Leave Rules applicable to employees of the Central Government. For members of the Administrative Staff, the said rules shall be aligned with the principles and provisions of the Leave Rules applicable to employees of the Government of Kerala.

28.2. Specific Leave Categories:

Rules governing specific categories of leave, such as study leave, sabbatical leave, and extraordinary leave, shall be as approved by the Executive Council.

28.3. Leave Not a Matter of Right:

Leave of any description cannot be claimed by an employee as a matter of right.

28.4. Discretion of Leave Sanctioning Authority:

Where the exigencies of the Institute's work so require, the authority empowered to grant leave reserves the absolute discretion to refuse or revoke leave of any description.

29. Recruitment and Promotion of Staff

29.1. Regulation of Recruitment and Promotion:

The recruitment and promotion, including assessment and merit promotion, in respect of all categories of staff of the Institute shall be regulated strictly in accordance with the recruitment and promotion rules as formulated and adopted by the Executive Council.

29.2. Annual Performance Appraisal Reports:

The Institute shall utilise Annual Performance Appraisal Reports (APARs) for all its employees as a component of the evaluation and review process.

29.3. Review of Employees:

A system for the periodic review of the employees of the Institute shall be established and maintained, in strict conformity with the provisions stipulated in Fundamental Rule 56(j).

30. Scheduled Caste Scheduled Tribes

30.1. Applicability of Reservation Rules:

The rules and orders issued by the Government of India from time to time regarding the reservation of posts for Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), Other Backward Classes (OBCs), and Persons with Disabilities shall be applicable to the extent permissible and relevant in respect of appointments to posts to be made in the Institute. In the event of any conflict between the rules and orders issued by the Government of India and those issued by the Government of Kerala concerning reservation, the provisions of Section 39 of these Rules and Regulations shall apply.

31. Contractual Services

31.1. Engagement of Contractual Services:

The Director is hereby empowered to engage external agencies or contractors for the provision of services related to infrastructural support, maintenance, and other ancillary services required by the Institute, to the extent deemed feasible and necessary for the efficient functioning thereof.

31.2. Status of Contractor's Personnel:

Any individual engaged by an external agency or contractor for the provision of services to the Institute pursuant to a contract executed under the provisions of clause 30.1 shall not, under any circumstances whatsoever, be construed as or deemed to be an employee of the Institute.

Miscellaneous

32. Execution of Contracts on Behalf of the Institute

32.1. Authority for Execution of Agreements and Contracts:

All agreements, contracts, and other instruments, save and except any agreement or contract entered into between the Institute and the Director, which may be requisite or necessary for the proper conduct of the business and affairs of the Institute, shall be executed on behalf of the Executive Council by the Director or by an officer of the Institute duly authorised by the Executive Council for the said purpose.

33. Perpetual Succession

33.1. Status and Legal Capacity:

The Institute shall possess perpetual succession and a common seal, and shall be capable of suing and being sued in its own name through its Registrar.

34. Amendments of the Rules and Regulations

34.1. Procedure for Amendment:

These Rules and Regulations may be amended. Any such amendment shall be effected only with the concurrence of the Department and after strict adherence to the due procedure enumerated in the respective statute by which the Institute is registered.

35. Residuary Power

35.1. Exercise of Residuary Powers:

Any powers not expressly vested or provided for in these Rules and Regulations may be exercised by the Executive Council in consultation with the Government.

35.2. Interpretation of Rules:

In the matter of interpretation of these Rules and Regulations or any provisions of the rules of governance of the Institute, the decision rendered by the Executive Council shall be final and binding.

35.3. Power to Relax Provisions:

The Executive Council is hereby vested with the competence to relax any or all of the provisions of these Rules and Regulations in the larger public interest.

36. Right to Information Act

36.1. Implementation of the Act:

The Institute shall undertake all necessary measures for the effective implementation of the provisions of the Right to Information Act, 2005.

36.2. Appointment of Officers:

In furtherance of the implementation of the said Act, the Institute shall appoint appropriate Public Information Officers, Appellate Authorities, and Transparency Officers as required by the provisions of the Act.

36.3. Proactive Disclosures:

The Institute shall diligently monitor compliance with the requirements of Section 4 of the Right to Information Act, 2005, and shall ensure that proactive disclosures are properly updated and revised periodically on the Institute's official platforms.

37. Implementation of Sexual Harassment Act

37.1. Compliance with the Act:

The Institute shall take all necessary measures to ensure the effective implementation of the provisions of The Sexual Harassment of Woman at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013, hereinafter referred to as "the Act," in its true spirit and form.

37.2. Establishment of Internal Committee:

In furtherance of the implementation of the Act, the Institute shall constitute an Internal Committee in accordance with the requirements stipulated in the Act, for the prevention and redressal of complaints of sexual harassment.

37.3. Adherence to Procedures:

The Institute shall ensure strict adherence to the procedures and guidelines prescribed under the Act for the prevention of sexual harassment and for the inquiry and redressal of complaints received thereunder.

38. Establishment of Grievances Redressal Mechanism

38.1. Requirement for Grievance Redressal Mechanism:

There shall be established and maintained a proper mechanism within the Institute for the redressal of grievances of the employees of the Institute.

38.2. Appointment of Grievance Redressal Committee:

The Institute shall appoint a Grievance Redressal Committee for the purpose of addressing and resolving grievance petitions in a time-bound manner.

38.3. Functions of the Committee:

The Grievance Redressal Committee shall be responsible for receiving, examining, and disposing of grievance petitions submitted by employees in accordance with the procedures and timelines as may be prescribed by the Executive Council.

39. Rules and Regulations

39.1. Adherence to These Rules and Regulations:

The Institute shall strictly adhere to the provisions set forth in these Rules and Regulations.

39.2. Framing of Additional Service Rules:

In addition to these Rules and Regulations, the Institute may frame additional service rules governing its activities. Such additional service rules shall, in all respects, be consistent with and not in contradiction to the provisions of these Rules and Regulations or any directives issued by the Government or the S&T Department.

39.3. Precedence:

In the event of any inconsistency or conflict between any additional service rules framed by the Institute and these Rules and Regulations, the provisions of these Rules and Regulations shall prevail.

40. Matters Not Covered Herein

40.1. Removal of Doubts and Interpretation:

Where any doubt arises as to the interpretation of any of the provisions contained within these Rules and Regulations, the matter shall be formally referred to the Executive Council for a definitive decision.

40.2. Matters not Covered:

If any question or matter arises which is not expressly covered by the provisions of these Rules and Regulations, the decision rendered by the Executive Council in respect thereof shall be final.

40.3. Recourse to Government Rules:

In regard to the regulation of any matter not covered hereinabove, recourse shall be taken to the relevant rules, orders, or directives issued by the Central Government on the subject for Academic Staff, and to the relevant rules, orders, or directives issued by the Government of Kerala on the subject for Administrative Staff.

41. Saving Clause

41.1. Binding Nature of Government Directives:

Notwithstanding anything contained in these Rules and Regulations, any directives issued by the Government shall be binding upon the Institute and shall have an overriding effect on any provision of these Rules and Regulations, the statutes governing the Institute, or any other rules of the Institute.
